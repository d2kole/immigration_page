Why Is a Form I-693 Immigration Medical Exam Required?

U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) mandates that Green Card and Adjustment-of-Status applicants submit a completed Form I-693, Report of Medical Examination and Vaccination Record, to confirm they are free from communicable diseases of public health significance.[USCIS Policy Manual]

The exam must be performed by a USCIS-designated civil surgeon. During the visit, the civil surgeon verifies required age-appropriate vaccines per CDC Technical Instructions, screens for tuberculosis and sexually transmitted infections, and certifies the results on Form I-693. Submitting a sealed I-693 with your I-485 avoids costly Request-For-Evidence (RFE) delays and keeps your Green Card timeline on track.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the immigration medical exam take?

The typical examination takes approximately 1-2 hours, depending on your medical history and vaccination needs. All laboratory tests are performed on-site, which helps streamline the process.

How soon will I receive my sealed Form I-693?

In most cases, if all required tests and vaccinations are completed during your visit, we can provide your sealed Form I-693 within 3-5 business days. Some tests may require additional time for results.

What vaccines are required for the immigration medical exam?

The required vaccines typically include: MMR (measles, mumps, rubella), Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis), Varicella (chickenpox), Polio, Influenza (seasonal), Pneumococcal, Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, Meningococcal, and Haemophilus influenzae type B. The specific vaccines required depend on your age, medical history, and previous vaccinations.

Do I need to fast before the exam?

Fasting is not typically required for the standard immigration medical exam. However, if additional tests are needed based on your medical history, fasting may be recommended. Our staff will provide specific instructions when you schedule your appointment.

How long is the Form I-693 valid?

According to USCIS guidelines, Form I-693 is valid for 2 years from the date the civil surgeon signs it. However, it's recommended to file it with USCIS within 60 days of the civil surgeon's signature for optimal processing.

Can I bring an interpreter to my appointment?

Yes, you may bring an interpreter if needed. However, our staff speaks English, Spanish, Haitian Creole, and French, so we can accommodate most patients without the need for an external interpreter.
